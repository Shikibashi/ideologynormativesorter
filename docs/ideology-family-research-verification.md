# Ideology family research verification

This pass extends the contested-label check by validating the broader ideology families and recurring traditions used in `src/data/labels.ts`. It does not change centroids; it records whether current naming/description strategy is consistent with academic usage and what should be reviewed later. Actionable centroid/UI follow-ups are tracked in `src/data/labelPrecisionFollowUps.ts`.

| Family / traditions checked | Representative project labels | Research baseline | Verification result | Follow-up |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Liberalism and market liberalism | Market Liberal, Classical Liberalism, Social Liberalism, Conservative Liberalism, Market-Governance Liberalism, Civil-Libertarian Cosmopolitan | Liberalism is a broad family centered on freedom, equality, rights, and limited/arbitrary-power-constraining institutions; market liberalism and social liberalism are distinct variants. | Current labels preserve the important split between classical/market liberal, social liberal, and technocratic market-governance liberal usage. The rename away from plain “Neoliberalism” remains appropriate because that term is contested and often polemical. | If more precision is needed, split late-20th-century neoliberal policy regimes from generic technocratic market governance. |
| Socialism and social democracy | Social Democrat, Democratic Socialist, Market Socialist, Revolutionary State Socialist, Marxist-Leninist, Maoism, Trotskyism, Council Communist, Syndicalist, Guild Socialism, Libertarian Socialism | Socialism covers multiple traditions of social ownership or democratic control of productive assets; social democracy is usually reformist welfare capitalism or gradualist socialism depending context, while democratic socialism should not be collapsed into Marxism-Leninism. | Current labels correctly keep social democracy, democratic socialism, state socialism, council communism, syndicalism, market socialism, and libertarian socialism separate. | Later centroid review should ensure Revolutionary State Socialist is not too close to Marxist-Leninist/Maoist labels. |
| Anarchism and libertarian currents | Anarcho-Capitalist, Mutualist, Left-Wing Market Anarchism, Individualist Anarchism, Anarcho-Communist, Techno-Anarchist / Crypto-Anarchist, Minarchist, Agorist, Panarchism | Anarchism generally rejects or deeply questions domination, hierarchy, and state authority; anarcho-capitalism is right-libertarian and contested as anarchism because it preserves strong private-property rights. | Current notes correctly warn that anarcho-capitalism is contested, while left-wing market anarchism, mutualism, socialist anarchism, minarchism, and agorism remain distinct. Techno-anarchism is appropriately marked emerging/noncanonical. | Consider future split between crypto-anarchist, peer-to-peer commons, and techno-libertarian variants. |
| Conservatism and Christian-democratic traditions | National Traditionalist, Christian Democrat, Paleoconservatism, One-Nation Conservatism, Conservative Liberalism, Integralism, Theocratic Politics | Conservatism emphasizes tradition, inherited institutions, social continuity, prudence, and skepticism toward rationalist redesign; Christian democracy draws on Christian social thought, subsidiarity, social market themes, and democratic institutions. | Current descriptions are broadly consistent and avoid collapsing Christian democracy into theocracy or national traditionalism. The Theocratic Politics caution note correctly distinguishes theocratic ordering from personal religion. | If adding Catholic Integralism as a more precise label, keep it separate from generic Christian democracy and generic theocracy. |
| Republicanism, communitarianism, democracy, and cosmopolitanism | Republicanism, Communitarianism, Radical Democracy, Liquid Democracy, Democratic Confederalism, World Federalism, Civil-Libertarian Cosmopolitan | Republicanism is a family concerned with self-government and freedom as non-domination; cosmopolitanism treats all persons as belonging to a wider moral community; democratic theories vary in how directly participation should operate. | Current labels are acceptable because they distinguish civic-republican non-domination, communitarian common goods, direct/participatory democracy, delegated/liquid democracy, confederal democracy, and global/cosmopolitan commitments. | Add future UI help if users confuse republicanism with a contemporary party label. |
| Nationalism and national self-determination | Civic Nationalist, Ethnonationalist, National Traditionalist, Political Zionism, Hindutva, Religious Nationalism, Welfare Chauvinism | Nationalism is not one doctrine; it can be civic, ethnic, religious, diasporic, anti-colonial, liberal, or exclusionary. Political Zionism and Hindutva need special care because each has internally diverse and contested usage. | Current labels are acceptable because civic nationalism and ethnonationalism remain separate, Political Zionism is scoped to Jewish national self-determination/statehood, and Hindutva is explicitly separated from Hinduism as a religion. | Future precision requires subtype splits for Zionism and Hindutva rather than one centroid each. |
| Populism | Right-Wing Populism, Left-Wing Populism, Agrarian Populism | Populism is commonly treated as a thin-centered ideology or political style opposing a pure people to a corrupt elite, though this definition is debated. | Current caution notes correctly frame populism as thin/style-like and avoid treating it as a full doctrine by itself. | Consider making the UI note visible wherever populist sublabels are displayed. |
| Fascism and authoritarian nationalist currents | Fascist / Palingenetic Ultranationalist, Eco-Fascism, Strasserite Fascism, National Bolshevism, Neoreactionary, Absolute Monarchist | Fascism is narrower than generic authoritarianism and is commonly analyzed through palingenetic ultranationalism, mass mobilization, hierarchy, anti-liberalism, and national rebirth. | Current naming and notes avoid the earlier overbreadth problem by not equating fascism with generic authoritarianism. Strasserite Fascism and Eco-Fascism are correctly treated as fascist variants, not socialism or ordinary environmentalism. | Future family-tree work may benefit from a clearer fascist subfamily under authoritarian labels. |
| Environmental ideologies | Ecomodernist, Ecosocialist, Degrowth Green, Deep Ecology, Bioregionalism, Eco-Authoritarianism, Eco-Fascism | Environmental political thought ranges from technology/growth-compatible ecomodernism to ecosocialism, degrowth, deep ecology, local/bioregional politics, and authoritarian/fascist ecological variants. | Current labels preserve major distinctions: ecomodernism is not degrowth or ecosocialism; eco-authoritarianism is not ordinary climate policy; eco-fascism is narrowly fascist/exclusionary environmental politics. | Future centroid review should ensure ecomodernism does not overfit generic technocracy. |
| Technocracy and emerging technology ideologies | Technocratic Centralist, Cyberocratic Governance, Transhumanism, Accelerationism, Singularitarianism-style aliases where present | Technocracy assigns authority to technical expertise; cyberocracy emphasizes information systems/networks; transhumanism emphasizes technological improvement of human capacities; accelerationism has incompatible left/right/tech variants. | Current labels are acceptable because emerging categories are marked as experimental or provisional, and accelerationism explicitly states the current centroid is technology-centered rather than universal. | Highest-priority future split: accelerationism into left, right/unconditional, and technology-centered variants. |
