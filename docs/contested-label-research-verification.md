# Contested label research verification

This note verifies the labels that were marked **Needs narrowing** or **Use cautiously** in the academic audit. It records the research baseline used for the current wording in `src/data/labels.ts` and flags centroid follow-ups separately from text fixes.

| Label id | Current display label | Research baseline | Verification result | Follow-up |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `revolutionary-collectivist` | Revolutionary State Socialist | SEP/IEP-style socialism references distinguish democratic, revolutionary, state-socialist, council, syndicalist, and libertarian socialist traditions. | Current wording is acceptable because it narrows the label to revolutionary politics where centralized public ownership or state power is central, while explicitly separating council, syndicalist, and libertarian socialist traditions. | Later centroid review should confirm this is not too close to Marxist-Leninist, Maoist, or council-communist variants. |
| `neoliberalism` | Market-Governance Liberalism | Academic usage of neoliberalism is contested, but commonly covers market-oriented governance through competition, privatization/outsourcing, expert institutions, and state-making for markets. | Current wording is acceptable because it avoids using “neoliberalism” as a catchall insult and describes the narrower market-governance sense. | Consider a future subtype split for late-20th-century policy-regime neoliberalism vs. technocratic market liberalism. |
| `cyberocracy` | Cyberocratic Governance | Ronfeldt/RAND usage treats cyberocracy as a proposed information-age governance form shaped by information systems, electronic communications infrastructure, and organizational change. | Current wording is acceptable because it presents cyberocracy as experimental governance through information systems and decision-support rather than a settled ideology equal to liberalism or socialism. | Centroid should remain provisional until algorithmic governance, cybernetic planning, and expert-technocratic variants are separated. |
| `zionism` | Political Zionism | Scholarly and historical usage treats Zionism as Jewish nationalism/self-determination or statehood in the Land of Israel, with liberal, socialist, religious, revisionist, territorial, and post-Zionist debates. | Current wording is acceptable because it names political Jewish self-determination/statehood without implying one view on borders, religion, minority rights, or current governments. | Future centroids should split liberal, labor/socialist, religious, and revisionist Zionist variants if this area is used for precise results. |
| `strasserism` | Strasserite Fascism | Fascism scholarship treats fascism as palingenetic ultranationalism; specialist accounts of Strasserism place it inside Nazism/fascism despite anti-capitalist rhetoric. | Current wording is acceptable because it classifies Strasserism as a fascist far-right current and warns that anti-capitalist rhetoric does not make it socialism academically. | Consider whether the subtype should be grouped under a broader fascist family if families are later normalized. |
| `islamic-democracy` | Islamic Democratic Constitutionalism | Scholarship describes Islamic democracy as contested: Muslim-majority electoral democracy, Islamic constitutionalism, or democracy constrained by Islamic law may all be meant. | Current wording is acceptable because it narrows the result to electoral government, constitutional limits, and public accountability within an Islamic ethical/legal framework while distinguishing theocracy. | Future split could separate Islamic constitutional democracy, Muslim democratic parties, and theocratic politics. |
| `accelerationism` | Accelerationism | Academic and intellectual-history usage treats accelerationism as a family of left, right, unconditional, and technology-centered positions that intensify capitalism, technology, or modernity to force transformation. | Current wording is acceptable as a stopgap because it explicitly says the centroid is closest to technology-centered accelerationism and is provisional until variants are split. | Must split into left, right/unconditional, and technology-centered accelerationist centroids for higher precision. |
| `techno-anarchism` | Techno-Anarchist / Crypto-Anarchist | Anarchist theory centers skepticism of authority/power; crypto-anarchist and cypherpunk literature emphasizes encryption, anonymity, decentralization, and resistance to state/corporate information control. | Current wording is acceptable because it frames the label as an emerging overlap among cypherpunk, crypto-anarchism, and networked anarchism rather than as a canonical school. | Future split could separate crypto-anarchist, peer-to-peer commons, and broader techno-libertarian variants. |

## Source notes

- Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy: liberalism (`https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/liberalism/`), socialism (`https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/socialism/`), anarchism (`https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/anarchism/`), nationalism (`https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/nationalism/`), conservatism (`https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/conservatism/`), republicanism (`https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/republicanism/`), and democracy (`https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/democracy/`).
- Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy: socialism (`https://iep.utm.edu/socialis/`) and political philosophy methodology (`https://iep.utm.edu/polphil/`).
- Roger Griffin / Oxford-style fascism scholarship for fascism as palingenetic ultranationalism and fascist-current classification; e.g. Griffin's `The Nature of Fascism` summary (`https://books.google.com/books/about/The_Nature_of_Fascism.html?id=544bouZiztIC`) and a concise overview (`https://www.libraryofsocialscience.com/ideologies/resources/griffin-the-palingenetic-core/`).
- David Ronfeldt/RAND and related information-society scholarship for cyberocracy (`https://www.rand.org/content/dam/rand/pubs/papers/2008/P7745.pdf`; `https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/01972243.1992.9960123`).
- Scholarly/historical Zionism references treating Zionism as Jewish national self-determination/statehood while recognizing internal variants; e.g. Alan Patten's Princeton-hosted paper discussing Jewish self-determination (`https://apatten.scholar.princeton.edu/document/46`) and historical overview (`https://www.fpri.org/article/2015/01/origins-and-evolution-of-zionism/`).
- Islamic constitutionalism and Islamic democracy scholarship emphasizing contested meanings and institutional design (`https://scholarship.richmond.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1158&context=law-faculty-publications`; `https://www.e-ir.info/2015/01/07/what-is-islamic-democracy-the-three-cs-of-islamic-governance/`).
- Accelerationism scholarship and intellectual history distinguishing left/right/technology-centered variants, including the `#Accelerate` tradition and secondary scholarship (`https://www.urbanomic.com/book/accelerate/`; `https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/0969725X.2019.1620464` where accessible).
- Cypherpunk and crypto-anarchism scholarship for the technology-mediated anarchist/current framing (`https://st-annes-mcr.org.uk/mcr-life/academic/staar/staar-7-2017/barret-bertelloni-2017-the-cypherpunk-vision-of-techno-politics/`; `https://www.researchgate.net/publication/380819410_Crypto-anarchy_a_paradigm_shift_for_society_and_the_legal_system`).
